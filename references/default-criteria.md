# Default Hackathon Judging Criteria

When hackathon-specific criteria are not available through research, use these defaults adapted to the hackathon's ecosystem.

## Universal Criteria (applicable to most hackathons)

### 1. Innovation & Creativity (Weight: 25%)
- Novelty of the idea
- Creative use of technology
- Unique approach to the problem
- Differentiation from existing solutions
- Score range: 1-10

### 2. Technical Implementation (Weight: 25%)
- Code quality and architecture
- Appropriate use of technologies
- Complexity of what was built
- Working demo vs slideware
- Use of required/sponsor APIs and SDKs
- Score range: 1-10

### 3. User Experience & Design (Weight: 15%)
- Intuitive interface
- Visual polish for a hackathon context
- User flow completeness
- Accessibility considerations
- Score range: 1-10

### 4. Impact & Usefulness (Weight: 20%)
- Does it solve a real problem?
- Size of the addressable market/audience
- Potential for real-world adoption
- Social or economic impact
- Score range: 1-10

### 5. Presentation & Demo (Weight: 15%)
- Clarity of pitch
- Demo quality and reliability
- Team's ability to explain technical decisions
- Handling of Q&A
- "Wow factor"
- Score range: 1-10

## Ecosystem-Specific Criteria

### Blockchain/Web3 Hackathons (Solana, Ethereum, etc.)
Additional considerations:
- On-chain integration depth (not just a wrapper)
- Smart contract security
- Decentralization level
- Token economics (if applicable)
- Composability with existing protocols
- Ecosystem contribution (tools, infra, public goods)

### AI/ML Hackathons
Additional considerations:
- Model selection and justification
- Training data quality and ethics
- Inference speed and cost
- Evaluation metrics
- Novel application of AI vs wrapper around an API

### Fintech Hackathons
Additional considerations:
- Regulatory awareness
- Security and compliance
- Financial model viability
- Risk management
- Integration with existing financial infrastructure

### Social Impact Hackathons
Additional considerations:
- Measurable impact potential
- Sustainability beyond the hackathon
- Community engagement approach
- Scalability to underserved populations
- Ethical considerations

## Scoring Calibration Guide

| Score | Meaning | Hackathon Context |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| 1-2   | Poor | Incomplete, broken, or irrelevant |
| 3-4   | Below Average | Basic attempt, significant gaps |
| 5-6   | Average | Functional but unremarkable, typical hackathon project |
| 7     | Good | Solid execution, stands out from the crowd |
| 8     | Very Good | Impressive for a hackathon, finalist material |
| 9     | Excellent | Outstanding, strong winner candidate |
| 10    | Exceptional | Best-in-class, memorable across hackathons |

Note: The median hackathon project scores around 5. A score of 7+ is genuinely impressive. Scores of 9-10 should be rare.
